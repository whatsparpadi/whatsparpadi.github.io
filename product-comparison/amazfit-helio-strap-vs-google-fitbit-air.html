Introduction - The Screenless Trend

Smartwatches have dominated our wrists for a decade, but a new trend is emerging: distraction-free wellness tracking. The Amazfit Helio Strap and the Google Fitbit Air are minimalist, screen-free fitness trackers. They skip display panels entirely, prioritizing ultra-light comfort, long battery life, and discrete, continuous data monitoring.

But while they share a screenless philosophy, they differ in sensor configurations, companion app models, and overall pricing structures. Let's look at how the Helio Strap and Google's Fitbit Air compare side-by-side.

The Philosophy of Screenless Design

Both wearables remove the constant notifications and screen-glance loops. They are built to collect metrics passively, sending data to your phone via Bluetooth. They lack watch faces, touch areas, and buttons.

The Amazfit Helio Strap is designed as a comfortable fabric band. It integrates with other Amazfit devices (like the Helio Ring) to build a unified recovery and sleep score in the Zepp ecosystem. The Google Fitbit Air is a tiny tracking capsule (weighing just 5.2 grams) that clips into silicone or textile bands. It is exceptionally small and vanishes on your wrist.

Battery Life & Charging Speeds

Without displays, both wearables outpace traditional smartwatches in battery life:

  • Amazfit Helio Strap: Offers up to 10 days of battery life under typical usage, capturing continuous heart rate metrics every single second.
  • Google Fitbit Air: Delivers up to 7 days of tracking. It compensates with Quick Charging, providing 1 full day of battery life in only 5 minutes of charging, and goes from 0 to 100% in 90 minutes.
The Helio Strap is best for long-duration trackers who want to charge their wearable only three times a month, while the Fitbit Air offers rapid top-ups.

Ecosystems, Apps & Sensor Precision

Your choice depends on the companion app and subscription model:

  • Amazfit Ecosystem: The Helio Strap uses the Zepp App, providing BioCharge™ recovery analysis, sleep metrics, and stress graphs. Crucially, no subscription fees are required to view your data. It also aggregates metrics with the Amazfit Helio Ring if you wear both.
  • Google Fitbit Ecosystem: The Fitbit Air integrates with the Google Health App (requiring a Google Account). It is packed with hardware, including a skin temperature sensor and a red/infrared SpO2 sensor for blood oxygen tracking. However, unlocking advanced metrics (like readiness scores and personalized sleep profiles) requires a Fitbit Premium subscription.

Pricing, Materials & Comfort

Comfort levels differ based on the band structures:

  • The Helio Strap uses a breathable, flexible fabric band designed for gym use. Worn day or night, it adjusts smoothly and retails at ₹8,999 in India.
  • The Fitbit Air features a recycled polycarbonate housing with multiple band styles (Silicone and Textile). Google even offers a Special Edition Stephen Curry Band. It is priced starting at $99.99 (approx. ₹8,499).

Final Verdict - Which Wearable Wins?

Buy the Amazfit Helio Strap if:

  • You want the longest possible battery life (up to 10 days).
  • You refuse to pay monthly subscriptions to view your recovery data.
  • You already own or plan to buy other Amazfit devices (smartwatch or Helio Ring) to use the Zepp ecosystem.
  • You prefer soft, breathable fabric straps right out of the box.

Buy the Google Fitbit Air if:

  • You want an extremely tiny, modular tracker (only 5.2g) that fits various band styles.
  • You require advanced hardware features like skin temperature variation and blood oxygen (SpO2) sensors.
  • You already use Google Health and prefer Google's analytics.
  • You want quick charging to top up 1 day of battery in 5 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

A screenless fitness tracker has no display panel, buttons, or watch face, using your phone's companion app instead. It is chosen to eliminate screen distractions, improve battery life, and maintain a lightweight form factor.

The Amazfit Helio Strap provides up to 10 days of battery life under typical usage, while the Google Fitbit Air offers up to 7 days on a single charge.

The Google Fitbit Air includes an optical heart rate monitor, a 3-axis accelerometer and gyroscope, red and infrared sensors for SpO2 tracking, and a skin temperature sensor.
